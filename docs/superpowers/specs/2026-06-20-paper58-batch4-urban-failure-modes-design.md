# Paper58 Batch 4 Urban Failure Modes Design

## Purpose

Batch 4 is a targeted external Tier 1 experiment, not a manuscript-strengthening shortcut. It tests whether the Batch 2 `xiong_an_fringe_holdout` failure is reproducible in similar urban-fringe settings and whether the smaller `suzhou_fringe_holdout` spatial-offset pattern appears elsewhere.

## Design

Batch 4 contains `10` new 2020-2021 strict Tier 1 urban candidates that are separate from Batch 1, Batch 2, and Batch 3 area names.

- `7` `xiong_an_like` candidates emphasize new-area or urban-wetland/urban-open-land edge settings where substantial `5/7 -> 11` or similar class-`11` transition pressure may occur.
- `3` `suzhou_like` candidates emphasize dense urban edges where small spatial offsets may matter even if the class-`11` representation failure is less extreme.

All rows keep `development_contact_status = none` with explicit no-contact evidence. The batch should be evaluated independently before any pooled interpretation.

## Stop/Go Rule

Batch 4-only is the next primary readout after acquisition and prediction. If Batch 4 fails, do not hide the failure by pooling. If it passes, compare it against Batch 2 and Batch 3 before changing manuscript claims.

## Non-Goals

- Do not change benchmark thresholds.
- Do not change the dynamics model.
- Do not edit the manuscript.
- Do not treat urban as one homogeneous stratum; preserve the `xiong_an_like` and `suzhou_like` labels in notes and interpretation.
